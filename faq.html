Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers about Step Higher, Nella’s Place, tours, scholarships, and how to get involved.

What is Step Higher and how is Nella’s Place connected?
Step Higher runs college readiness programs and scholarships. Nella’s Place is our licensed group home for girls 8 to 21. Programs share staff, training, and a common mission.
Who can join a Step Higher college tour?
Middle and high school students with parent or guardian consent. Priority goes to foster youth and low income students.
How do I apply for a tour?
Go to the Tours page, select a trip, complete the form, and watch your email for deadlines and required forms.
What costs are covered on tours?
Usually bus, hotel, campus visits, and most meals. Personal snacks and souvenirs are not covered. Need based support may reduce or remove fees.
Do tours include HBCUs?
Yes. HBCUs are a core part of our tour routes along with a mix of public and private universities.
What is the supervision and safety plan on trips?
Staff to student ratios meet or exceed state guidelines. We do headcounts, room checks, and use emergency contact trees and GPS bus tracking.
How do SHNP scholarships work?
Annual awards for graduating seniors and current college students who have engaged with our programs. Criteria include need, participation, academics, and a short essay.
What materials are required for scholarship applications?
Application form, transcript or recent grade report, short essay, and one recommendation. See the Scholarships page for exact dates.
Can awards be used for trade or certificate programs?
Yes, many awards allow accredited trade schools and certificate programs. Check each award description for details.
What is Nella’s Place?
A CARF accredited group home in Cincinnati providing 24 by 7 care, education support, therapy coordination, and life skills training for girls 8 to 21.
How do referrals to Nella’s Place work?
Referrals come from counties, case managers, or licensed professionals. Start through the Contact page or call the main line to check availability and begin intake.
What happens during intake?
We review placement paperwork, medical history, medication list, education records, clothing inventory, and care plans. Families and guardians are included when applicable.
How are medications managed?
Only trained staff handle medications under physician orders. Doses are logged, meds are stored securely, and prescribers are consulted for changes.
Do residents attend local schools?
Yes. We coordinate enrollment and transportation. If an IEP or 504 plan exists, we partner with the school district to support it.
Are therapy and medical appointments coordinated?
We schedule and coordinate therapy, primary care, dental, vision, and specialist visits with approved providers and keep case managers updated.
How can I volunteer?
Apply on the Volunteer page for tutoring, chaperoning, events, or admin support. Background checks are required for roles with youth.
Do you offer group volunteer days?
Yes. Companies, colleges, and community groups can schedule service days like supply drives or campus prep projects.
How can I donate?
Use the Donate page to give online or learn about corporate matching, sponsorships, and in kind gifts such as school supplies or hygiene kits.
Can donors sponsor a tour seat?
Yes. You can fully or partially sponsor a seat. We will confirm the route and share impact updates after the trip.
Is Step Higher a 501c3 and will I get a receipt?
Yes. Step Higher is a registered nonprofit. Donation receipts are emailed automatically and are tax deductible as allowed by law.
What safety standards does Nella’s Place follow?
State licensing rules, CARF standards, staff training, 24 by 7 supervision, incident reporting, and regular audits for quality and safety.
Do you provide language access or ADA support?
Yes. Interpreter services and accessibility accommodations are available with advance notice. Tell us what you need on the form.
How is privacy handled for youth information?
We follow state rules and best practices for data protection, need to know access, and secure storage. See the Privacy Policy for details.
Where are you located and how do I reach you?
1610 California Ave, Cincinnati, OH 45237. Email info@shnp.org or call 513 436 0118. See the Contact page for hours.
How can I stay updated on programs and dates?
Join our email list on the Contact or Blog page and follow our social channels for tour dates, scholarship cycles, and events.
Do you accept internships or work study students?
Yes. We partner with local colleges for internships and work study. Check the Careers page or contact us with your program requirements.
