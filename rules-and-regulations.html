Rules & Regulations

A College Tour is an exciting opportunity whether you are a frequent traveler or venturing from home for the first time. Tours provide students with a chance to explore new people and places. Because of these experiences and for your safety, Step Higher College Tours has set some important rules and guidelines to make your trip more pleasurable.

*** Please be mindful that these are the minimal guidelines and expectations for your tour. The Tour Director or Group Leaders may provide additional policies. Severe infractions may result in a call to your parents or being sent home at the parent's expense.

🚌 Bus Etiquette

Buses must be kept in immaculate condition. Dispose of trash properly, keep noise minimal, and use headphones. Walking on the bus should be minimal. You will be held responsible for any damage you cause.

🏨 Hotel Behavior

Treat your hotel room with respect. Keep belongings organized, avoid loud noises, and do not leave hotel grounds without permission. You are responsible for any damage caused during your stay.

🔍 Lost or Stolen Items

You are responsible for your own belongings. Keep valuables with you at all times and report missing items to Chaperones immediately.

👥 Groups

Never go anywhere alone. Stay in groups of at least three or four. Always be on time for scheduled activities and departures.

🪪 Emergency Cards

Always carry your Step Higher Tours “Emergency Contact Card” in case you get lost or separated from the group.

👨‍👩‍👧 Family Visits

Visitors are limited to the hotel lobby. Curfew for visits is 10:00pm.

⏰ Curfew

All students must be in their hotel rooms at curfew time. Chaperones will conduct room checks nightly.

🚗 Motor Vehicles

Tour participants may not use any motorized vehicle other than the provided motor coach or a chaperone's vehicle.

🍷 Contraband

Possession or use of alcohol, controlled substances, or weapons will result in immediate dismissal from the tour at the parent's expense.

🤝 Civility

Show courtesy, maintain safety, and use good judgment. You are responsible for your belongings and behavior at all times.
